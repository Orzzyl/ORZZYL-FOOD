STEAKS

Sink your teeth into the rich, juicy perfection of a mouth - watering steak, seared to a golden-brown crust on the outside , tender and flavorful on the side. Whether you prefer it medium-rare or well-done, each bite delivers bold, smoky goodness and savory satisfaction. Paired with a side of buttery mashed potatoes or grilled veggies, it's more than a meal - it's a celebration of flavor

SIDES & APPETIZERS

Start your meal with a burst of bold Nigerian flavor. From spicy peppered snails to golden puff-puff, every bite is a delicious invitation. Dig into a flaky chicken pie or a scoop of creamy coleslaw, then pair it with rich moin-moin or sweet fried plantains. These sides don’t just complement your meal — they steal the spotlight. Every dish is crafted to awaken your taste buds and set the stage for what’s to come. Whether you’re sharing or savoring solo, the flavor is unforgettable.

DESSERTS

Finish strong with a sweet, golden doughnut — soft, fluffy, and lightly dusted with sugar. Each bite is warm, comforting, and perfectly indulgent, whether enjoyed on its own or dipped in chocolate or honey. Pair it with chilled zobo jelly, fruity tiger nut parfait, or a scoop of creamy vanilla ice cream for an extra layer of delight. Our dessert menu celebrates simple Nigerian favorites with rich textures and nostalgic flavors.

MAIN DISH

Dive into the heart of Nigerian cuisine with rich, hearty main dishes that comfort and satisfy. From smoky, spicy Jollof rice served with juicy grilled chicken to flavorful egusi soup loaded with assorted meats and scooped up with soft, warm pounded yam, every plate tells a story of tradition and taste. Whether it’s a spicy stew or a savory soup, our main courses are crafted to fill your belly and warm your soul — because in Nigeria, food isn’t just a meal, it’s an experience.

Chef Spotlight

Chef Oyeleke Oreoluwa

Head of Curlinary & Restaurant

A Nigerian Institute of Hospitality and Tourism Management-trained culinary specialist and Lagos Restaurant Week Award winner, Chef Oyeleke Oreoluwa brings a unique fusion of Yoruba culinary traditions and culinary expertise to Terra Kulture Restaurant. With a background in hospitality management, including experience at premium hotels in Victoria Island and Ikoyi, she seamlessly blends customer service excellence with innovative culinary creations. As Executive Chef at Terra Kulture, Oyeleke Oreoluwa leads kitchen operations and front-of-house coordination, ensuring exceptional guest experiences and operational excellence. Her culinary journey began with traditional Nigerian cuisine training at the Hotel Management Institute, Abuja, where she showcased contemporary Nigerian dishes at national food festivals, mastering indigenous spice blending and local ingredient sourcing and developing a deep appreciation for authentic Nigerian flavors with modern presentation. Under her guidance, Terra Kulture's culinary operations have achieved a 40% increase in customer satisfaction scores. She mentors her team to understand that exceptional food service requires not only culinary skill but also attention to detail and teamwork. Her unique perspective as both a certified wine specialist and former hotel operations manager continues to drive innovation and excellence across all restaurant departments.
